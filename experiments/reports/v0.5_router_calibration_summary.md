## Interpretation

- Under deterministic generation, block configurations do not produce measurable per-sample utility gains on this benchmark. Baseline, general, math, code, and always-all all score 0.778; chinese scores 0.767.

- Oracle per-sample routing does not improve over baseline because no non-baseline configuration beats baseline on any sample. Ties are resolved toward fewer active blocks, so oracle selects baseline for all 45 samples.

- Validation-calibrated routing also selects baseline for every category. Its lower 0.696 score reflects the held-out split being harder than the full aggregate, not a learned block advantage.

- This shows that, under deterministic generation, the current parameter blocks do not provide stable utility beyond the baseline. In this setting, the primary bottleneck is not router calibration: oracle routing also selects baseline for all samples.

- The v0.4 `Only chinese` gain may be sampling-sensitive and must be rechecked with matched generation settings and saved outputs before it is treated as a stable mechanism result.
